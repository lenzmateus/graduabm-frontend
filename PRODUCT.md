# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Active military personnel (soldiers, fire cadets) studying for highly competitive career advancement exams (CTSP - Curso Técnico de Segurança Pública, and CBA - Curso Básico de Administração). They operate under high professional pressure, rigorous service schedules, and intense time constraints, requiring highly efficient study sessions and clear feedback on their mastery.

## Product Purpose
To provide an elite, tactical, and distraction-free learning ecosystem. Success is measured by the student's mastery of the fire department's regulatory framework, legislation, and operational procedures, driving high retention (via active recall/SRS flashcards and cyclic study blocks) and mock-exam success.

## Brand Personality
Tactical, Direct, Operational — **technical and motivational** (the two are equal pillars). The tone is authoritative and disciplined, treating the user like a professional cadet, and it always points to the mission — approval: it motivates by raising the standard, acknowledging effort and showing progress, **never by empty cheerleading**. No decorative fluff, no hype/coddling, no corporate filler. For *how* this voice is written across UI, e-mail and push, see **[VOICE.md](VOICE.md)** (the editorial source of truth).

## Anti-references
- **Playful consumer apps**: Avoid colorful gradients, soft or playful consumer illustrations, emojis, rounded borders > 16px, and overly casual gamification.
- **SaaS-cream dashboards**: No beige/white clean cards, soft shadows, or bloated, decorative metrics.
- **Dry bureaucracy**: It must not look like an uninspired, dry government table. It must feel like an operational tactical terminal.

## Design Principles
1. **Operational Necessity Only**: If a component, metric, or visual asset does not directly help the user study, track their progress, or understand their standing, it must be removed.
2. **Hierarchy Over Ornamentation**: Use high contrast, strong typography scales (Bebas Neue for dominant data/statistics vs. Inter for UI), and distinct badges (IBM Plex Mono) to create extreme clarity of importance.
3. **Continuous Rotation**: The UI should encourage constant progression through rodadas (study rounds) and study cycles, removing execution friction rather than adding configuration gates.
4. **Distraction-Free Focus**: High-stakes testing modes (like the simulated exams) must enforce a clean, immersive environment that focuses exclusively on the countdown and response inputs.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
- High color-contrast ratios on deep charcoal background surfaces (meeting WCAG 2.1 AA targets).
- Support for rapid keyboard/numeric navigation for simulated exam grids and flashcard cards.
- Respect reduced-motion preferences, ensuring transitions are limited to subtle hover/active states without distracting entry animations.
